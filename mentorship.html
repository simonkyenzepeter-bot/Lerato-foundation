Guiding, Empowering, and Transforming Students, Youth, and Communities

Introduction

The Lerato Foundation Mentorship Program is a structured initiative designed to guide, empower, and transform individuals through personalized mentorship, training, and community engagement. We mentor students in institutions, youth in transition, and entire communities, ensuring holistic growth that nurtures leaders, strengthens families, and builds resilient societies.

1. Program Objectives

  • Provide academic and career guidance for students in schools, colleges, and universities.
  • Equip youth with leadership, entrepreneurship, and life skills necessary for personal and professional growth.
  • Mentor communities towards social responsibility, unity, and sustainable development.
  • Bridge the gap between generations, fostering knowledge transfer and shared values.
  • Raise a generation of leaders with integrity, compassion, and vision.

2. Target Groups

a) Students in Institutions

  • Secondary and university students.
  • Focus on academic success, scholarship access, exam preparation, and career planning.
  • Guidance on peer pressure, mental health, and confidence building.

b) Youth

  • Young men and women transitioning into adulthood.
  • Focus on life skills, entrepreneurship, leadership, employment readiness, and financial literacy.
  • Guidance on making positive choices and navigating challenges.

c) Communities

  • Local groups, families, and grassroots organizations.
  • Focus on empowerment, teamwork, conflict resolution, and community development projects.
  • Mobilizing collective action for change and strengthening social bonds.

3. Program Components

a) One-on-One Mentorship

Personalized sessions where mentors guide mentees in setting goals, overcoming challenges, and tracking progress.

b) Group Mentorship & Workshops

Interactive sessions on leadership, communication, digital skills, problem-solving, and social responsibility.

c) Academic & Career Guidance

  • Scholarship and internship support.
  • Career talks with professionals.
  • CV writing, interview preparation, and entrepreneurship pathways.

d) Peer-to-Peer Mentorship

Empowering trained mentees to mentor peers in schools, youth groups, and communities, creating a ripple effect.

e) Community Engagement Projects

  • Environmental conservation initiatives.
  • Health and wellness campaigns.
  • Education and literacy programs.
  • Social inclusion activities.

4. Program Approach

  1. Recruitment & Selection: Identify mentees and match them with mentors.
  2. Orientation: Training and induction for mentors and mentees.
  3. Mentorship Sessions: Regular one-on-one and group meetings.
  4. Skills Training: Workshops, seminars, and online learning.
  5. Evaluation: Continuous tracking of growth and mentor feedback.
  6. Graduation & Transition: Recognize mentees and empower them to mentor others.

5. Expected Outcomes

  • Students excelling academically and accessing scholarships.
  • Youth developing leadership skills, securing jobs, and starting businesses.
  • Communities strengthened through unity, service, and sustainability.
  • A ripple effect of empowered individuals becoming mentors to others.

6. How to Get Involved

  • As a Student/Youth: Apply to join as a mentee.
  • As a Mentor: Volunteer to guide the next generation.
  • As a Partner: Support through funding, resources, or collaboration.
